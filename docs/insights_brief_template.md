# Insights Brief

## Project

Patient Discharge Experience & Follow-Up Support Survey Insights Dashboard

## Reporting Period

07/01/2024-06/30/2025

## Data Sources

- CMS HCAHPS Hospital patient survey data for Florida hospitals
- Qualtrics follow-up survey design documentation

No actual Qualtrics response export is included in this repository. The Qualtrics component is a follow-up survey design informed by CMS HCAHPS findings.

## Key Measures Reviewed

| Dashboard category | Favorable-response definition | Survey-weighted favorable % |
| --- | --- | ---: |
| Discharge Information | `Yes` rows | 84.27 |
| Nurse Communication | `Always` rows | 76.39 |
| Doctor Communication | `Always` rows | 74.82 |
| Medicine Communication | `Always` rows | 58.01 |
| Overall Rating | `9 or 10` rows | 69.07 |
| Recommendation | `Definitely yes` rows | 70.01 |

## Summary

Florida HCAHPS results show comparatively strong discharge information performance and moderate-to-strong nurse and doctor communication. Medicine communication is the clearest opportunity area, especially explanations of possible medication side effects.

## Observations

- Discharge information is the strongest major category, with an 84.27% survey-weighted favorable-response rate.
- Nurse and doctor communication are similar overall, with nurse communication at 76.39% and doctor communication at 74.82%.
- Medicine communication trails the other major communication categories at 58.01%.
- The lowest specific submeasure is staff explaining possible side effects, at 43.78% survey-weighted favorable response.
- Overall rating and definite recommendation are moderate, at 69.07% and 70.01%, respectively.

## Follow-Up Support Themes

- Medication clarity after discharge
- Understanding possible side effects
- Knowing who to contact with medication or recovery questions
- Clarity and usability of discharge instructions

## Suggested Qualtrics Follow-Up Questions

- Before leaving the hospital, how clear were the explanations about possible medication side effects?
- Did you understand what each new medication was for?
- Did you know who to contact if you had medication questions after leaving?
- Did you feel confident following your care instructions at home?
- What would have made your discharge instructions or medication guidance clearer?

## Reporting Notes

Dashboard headline metrics use survey-volume-weighted favorable-response percentages. Facility comparison views use simple facility-level averages for benchmarking. Original CMS rows, non-favorable response buckets, and footnote fields remain available in the cleaned data and workbook for auditability.
